# Field Clerk Draft — 25A312 (Trump v. Cook)

## SR framing — the doctrinal punchline

Cook is Jacobson's shape at constitutional altitude. Same structural move:

**Congress legislates against a settled backdrop → the backdrop terms carry their common-law weight → the presumption controls unless a clear-and-convincing signal displaces it.**

In Jacobson (LRD-003, [[24-1990]]), the backdrop was *judicial review is presumed available*; § 4208's "notwithstanding any other law, no court shall have jurisdiction" was the clear-and-convincing displacer. In Cook, the backdrop is *fixed-term + for-cause statutes require pre-termination notice and hearing* (Reagan/Shurtleff); nothing in § 242 comes close to displacing it. The presumption stands; Cook wins on the narrowest available ground.

**This is the third case in the cohort where a rebuttable-presumption-plus-backdrop analysis is the load-bearing move.** With Erdemir's "true nature" doctrine as a functional cousin (label-vs-function is a presumption too), we now have enough datapoints to promote the category from "candidate" to **named war-chest doctrine**: *backdrop rebuttable presumption*.

## The move worth learning — "cause" as centuries-loaded phrase

Two words. Two centuries of common-law weight. The majority's move is to make that weight legible:

> "Congress enacted this statute against the backdrop of the common law, such that we must look to the common law to decipher what 'cause' (a term of art) requires."

The government wanted "cause" = anything the President finds concerning. Cook wanted "cause" = the specific statutory formulae Congress used elsewhere (inefficiency, neglect of duty, malfeasance). Both extremes lose. The majority instead reads "cause" as **a common-law term of art with a substantial threshold, contoured by the nature of the office**.

**Discipline for JR:** statutory terms with common-law pedigrees are never plain-English. "Cause," "reasonable," "true nature," "arbitrary and capricious," "just and equitable" — all of them carry their own history. The reader's job is to know which weight-of-words terms live in a text, and load them fully before analyzing.

Cross-stitch: This is exactly the [[24-2242|Erdemir]] discipline (label-vs-function; "true nature" doctrine) applied to statutory terms rather than pleadings. Same P13 move, different scale.

## The move to name as a category — historical-tradition safe harbor

Companion case [[25-332|Slaughter]] announces the general rule (Article II → at-will removal of principal executive officers). Cook activates a specific carveout: *the Federal Reserve is preserved as constitutional because it "follows in the distinct historical tradition" of the First and Second Banks of the United States*.

**Structural shape:** general rule + historical-tradition carveout. This is a new (or newly explicit) doctrinal category that will recur. Analogous shape: *Rahimi*'s Second-Amendment "consistent with the principles that underpin the historical tradition" test. Same doctrinal shell — rigid modern rule + flexible founding-era safe harbor.

**Watch:** the majority does NOT theorize the carveout. What earns historical-tradition protection? What is the test? Cook and Slaughter together preserve only the Federal Reserve (and reserve non-Article III courts). Every other independent agency claiming similar protection has to build its own historical-pedigree case. The FDIC and OCC are the first likely litigation targets. Barrett's Cook dissent flags the instability: "history used to support both a categorical rule and a carveout" is unstable footing.

## Hypothesis touches

- **H1** (court reasoning maps to LR Gate I/O/D): **confirmed at three levels.** Reviewability (Lane I — identifying that "cause" is subject to judicial line-drawing); "cause"-as-term-of-art (Lane O — operating the common-law backdrop test on the statutory text); notice-hearing derivation (Lane D — deriving the procedural requirement from the *Reagan/Shurtleff* backdrop). Three lane characters in a single opinion, at constitutional altitude.
- **H2** (Sufficient-Assumption conditional-direction appears in standing/limitations): **confirmed.** Conditional-direction move at Part III-A: *if* Congress legislates against the backdrop, *then* the backdrop terms control. Direction: forward-loading (backdrop → conclusion). Compare Jacobson's identical forward-loading; Erdemir's mixed forward/backward.
- **H3** (court "we need not decide" mirrors "too simple to be right"): **confirmed.** "We need not fully demarcate the contours of 'cause' today." Also "we do not resolve" the underlying Article II constitutionality question. Two reservations in one opinion — the smallest-claim-that-entails move is deliberate and repeated.
- **H4** (function-first / name-light in legal domains): **confirmed.** The "cause" analysis is entirely function-first — the common-law function of the term overrides both the government's plain-English reading AND Cook's statutory-analogue reading. Name-light because neither party's proposed name for "cause" is adopted; the function drives.

## Candidate war-chest additions (from this pass)

1. **Backdrop rebuttable presumption — PROMOTE TO NAMED WAR-CHEST DOCTRINE.** Three data points now (Jacobson at jurisdiction; Erdemir at pleading; Cook at constitutional-statutory interface). The shape: *general rule → backdrop displacement condition → threshold-of-signal test → conclusion*. The doctrinal domain sets the threshold (clear-and-convincing for judicial review; substantial-cause for removal; clear-statement for federalism). **Calibrating the threshold to the domain is still the master skill.**
2. **Historical-tradition safe harbor** — general rule + historical-tradition carveout doctrinal shape. Federal Reserve preserved; non-Article III courts reserved. Analog: Rahimi's Second-Amendment historical-tradition test. Watch for it in future structural-Constitution cases.
3. **Equity protects de facto officer pending litigation** — *In re Sawyer* and *White v. Berry* cabined to their facts (final equitable relief where legal remedies adequate). Interim reinstatement injunctions permissible. This restores preliminary-injunction practice for wrongfully-removed officers that had been in real doubt after *Wilcox* and *Boyle*.
4. **Notice adequacy — three-part rule.** Notice must include (i) some explanation of evidence at issue, (ii) some avenue for response, (iii) a deadline. Trump's social-media post fails all three. Analogized to *Mullane v. Central Hanover Bank*. This bridges statutory-notice doctrine into procedural-due-process territory.

## Carry this forward

> When you see fixed-term + for-cause in a removal statute, load it as a *Reagan/Shurtleff* backdrop presumption — notice and hearing are implicit until Congress speaks clearly to displace them. When you see "cause" or any common-law-pedigreed term, load it as a term of art with centuries of weight. The presumption + backdrop pattern is doing more work in constitutional doctrine than its low profile suggests.
