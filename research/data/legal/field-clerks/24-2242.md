# Field Clerk Draft — 24-2242 (Erdemir v. ITC)

## SR framing — the doctrinal punchline

Read past the antidumping-duty machinery. What Erdemir gives us — for SR practice — is **one principle wearing three different opinions**: once Congress sets a timing framework, litigants cannot escape it by (a) reframing the challenge, (b) invoking residual jurisdiction, or (c) seeking backward-looking reassessment through a forward-looking review mechanism.

Three procedural attempts, one substantive answer. That symmetry is the SR gold.

## The move worth learning — "true nature" as P13 at doctrinal scale

Erdemir framed his third-appeal challenge as a challenge to the ITC's *denial of reconsideration*. That framing had a purpose: it kept the challenge inside § 1581(i)'s residual jurisdiction (which had no 30-day timing bar), instead of inside § 1581(c) (which did).

Taranto's move, joined unanimously by Cunningham and Stark: **look past the pleading label to what the challenge is actually doing.** In its "true nature," Erdemir's challenge was to the 2016 material-injury determination itself — the thing that could have been challenged in a 30-day § 1581(c) filing. The re-labeling did not change what the challenge was.

**This is [[P13]] at doctrinal scale.** The label ("challenging the reconsideration denial") named one thing; the function ("undo the 2016 determination retroactively") was another. When they diverge, function wins.

Cross-stitch: In [[21-1729]], Taranto *dissents* to defend a label (significant-figure convention) against a functionally-motivated rewrite. Same judge, same discipline of scope-drawing, opposite direction — because there, the label was drafted deliberately with knowledge of the function, so it was doing real work. **The rule that emerges: function overrides label when the label was drafted before the function was understood; label overrides function when the label was drafted deliberately with knowledge of the function.** JR — practice this test on any P13 dispute you encounter.

## The candidate war-chest name — "the clock starts when you can see the shape, not when you can prove the substance"

This is the procedural mirror of [[25-1807]]'s inquiry-notice rule. In Insulet, smoke starts the DTSA statute of limitations — a plaintiff who *could reasonably have suspected* misappropriation cannot wait for proof. Here, in Erdemir, the same epistemic threshold appears at the procedural-timing level: a plaintiff who *knew of the conditional basis* for a future challenge (Çolakoğlu's parallel case) cannot wait for the ripening; the 30-day § 1581(c) window starts when the shape is visible, even if the substance isn't yet provable.

**Same suspicion → same clock** across substantive law (25-1807) and procedural law (24-2242). Candidate for war-chest promotion once we see it fire in a third case.

## Play Set — 24-2242

- [x] **P13 — map by function, not label.** "True nature" doctrine collapses the pleading label; function of the challenge is what routes it to the correct jurisdictional subsection.
- [x] **L6 — when mixed, split.** The panel splits sunset review, CCR, and reconsideration into three separate holdings even though they share one principle. Splitting the doctrinal moves lets each affirmance carry its own reasoning surface without contaminating the others.
- [x] **L4 — hexis, not situation.** Erdemir's failure is *hexis*-shaped — the habitual pattern of "wait for the parallel case to resolve" is what the ruling refuses. Timing rules test disposition, not one-time judgment.
- [x] **Sufficient_Assumption_anchor.** The "true nature" analysis works as a Sufficient Assumption: assume that pleading-label does not control jurisdictional routing → the § 1581(i) escape hatch closes. Plug it in; the ruling follows.
- [ ] P1 — present but not central.

## Hypothesis touches

- **H1** (court reasoning maps to LR Gate I/O/D): **confirmed.** § 1581(c) vs. (i) analysis is Lane I (identifying which jurisdictional subsection the challenge belongs to); *ARP Materials* protective-filing precedent is Lane O (operating the rule on facts); the "no unyielding jurisdictional hierarchy" *Ruhrgas* invocation is Lane D (deriving that sequencing is not required).
- **H2** (Sufficient-Assumption conditional-direction appears in standing/limitations): **confirmed.** Multiple conditional-direction moves — if reframing controls jurisdiction, then §1581(i) applies; if function controls, then §1581(c) applies. The court's choice of conditional-direction is the ruling.
- **H3** (court "we need not decide" mirrors "too simple to be right"): **confirmed.** The "may (perhaps must)" reservation in the sunset review holding is exact — panel resolves the case without committing to the fuller doctrinal move. Third data point on H3 after 25-1807's three declinations.

## Candidate war-chest additions (from this pass)

1. **"True nature" doctrine** — pleading-label does not control jurisdictional routing; function does.
2. **Protective filing + stay rule** — even conditional/contingent challenges must be timely filed to preserve the challenge; ripeness is assessed at adjudication, not at filing.
3. **"Clock starts when you can see the shape, not when you can prove the substance"** — cross-doctrinal principle uniting inquiry notice (25-1807) with §1581(c) timing (24-2242).
4. **Best-reading construction (post-Loper Bright)** — Federal Circuit doing its own statutory interpretation on trade-remedy statutes rather than deferring; SAA (§ 3512(d)) retains authoritative-canon weight.

## Carry this forward

> When you see a case with three procedural paths and one substantive answer, notice what the court is teaching about timing frameworks generally. The specific doctrine (§ 1581(c) 30-day window) is fungible; the principle (Congress's timing choice cannot be escaped by re-labeling) travels.
